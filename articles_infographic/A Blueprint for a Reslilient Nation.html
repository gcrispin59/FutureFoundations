The Current Landscape: A Siloed Approach

Today, our key disaster agencies possess incredible strengths but often operate in silos. This fragmentation leads to inefficiency, duplication of effort, and critical delays when every second counts.

FEMA

The Federal Coordinator

Strengths:

  • National Strategic Planning
  • Grant Program Management
  • Incident Command System (ICS)

Challenges:

  • Bureaucratic Hurdles
  • Reliance on Contractors
  • Vulnerable to Political Shifts

National Guard

The Domestic Force

Strengths:

  • Rapid Call-Up & Deployment
  • Robust Logistics & Transport
  • Trained, Dual-Use Equipment

Challenges:

  • State-Controlled Variability
  • Limited Federal Funding for Missions
  • Potential for Duplication

U.S. Army Corps (USACE)

The Engineering Backbone

Strengths:

  • Large-Scale Infrastructure Expertise
  • Hydrological Modeling
  • Long-Term Mitigation Planning

Challenges:

  • Extensive Project Timelines
  • Faces NIMBY Opposition
  • Perceived as Slow to React

From Silos to Synergy: A New Model

By breaking down walls and fostering deep collaboration, we transform costly duplication into powerful synergy. An integrated approach allows for shared resources, real-time data flow, and unified command, drastically improving outcomes.

Problem: Duplication & Inefficiency

Separate chains of command and uncoordinated resource allocation lead to wasted funds and delayed response times, increasing both human and economic costs.

Solution: Integrated Synergy

A unified model optimizes spending, enhances operational speed, and focuses resources on proactive mitigation, delivering greater protection for every dollar invested.

The Integrated Model in Action

Overcoming Hurdles: The USACE Advantage

A major barrier to long-term resilience is local politics blocking vital flood mitigation projects. Empowering USACE provides a science-based, federally-backed pathway to overcome this NIMBYism and build what's necessary.

The Cost of Political Gridlock

A significant percentage of vital hazard mitigation projects are delayed or cancelled due to local opposition, leaving communities dangerously exposed to predictable disasters.
